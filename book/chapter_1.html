Overview of Computer Programming and Problem Solving

A computer is an integral part of human civilization and the 21st century sees a giant step in its development.

Simply put, a computer is a machine that accepts an input and gives out an input. It uses some algorithms to process the input before giving an output. To communicate with a computer, we need to speak to it in a language it understands. Just like we have languages we use to communicate with ourselves, computers only understand 0 and 1 (machine language).

However, communicating with a computer using 0 and 1 could be hard for humans. Therefore we invented high-level languages like C, Java, Python, Scratch, etc.

First Principle of Thinking

Elon Musk, one of the most influential entrepreneurs of our time, often emphasizes the importance of "first principles" thinking when solving complex problems. This approach involves breaking down complex issues into their most fundamental, basic elements. By understanding the core components of a problem, one can develop more effective solutions rather than getting lost in the complexity.

In this course, we will take a similar first principles approach to computational problems. Rather than viewing problems in their full, intricate form, we will examine the basic building blocks - the principles - that comprise them. Scratch will serve as our primary tool for this bottom-up analysis.

By focusing on the essential pieces rather than the whole, we will gain critical insights into not just how but why certain computational problems function as they do. This foundation will enable us to design robust, creative solutions using Scratch's visual programming environment. Through hands-on learning with the building blocks of coding, we will become adept problem-solvers equipped to tackle complexity.
